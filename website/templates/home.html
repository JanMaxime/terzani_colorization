Tiziano Terzani

Tiziano Terzani (14 September 1938 â€“ 28 July 2004) was an Italian journalist and writer, best known for his extensive knowledge of 20th century East Asia and for being one of the very few western reporters to witness both the fall of Saigon to the hands of the Viet Cong and the fall of Phnom Penh at the hands of the Khmer Rouge in the mid-1970s.

Travels in Asia

Terzani spent a good part of his career traveling in Asia. He visited China, Japan, Korea, India, Laos, Cambogia, Birmania, Tibet, Nepal, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and many other countries
